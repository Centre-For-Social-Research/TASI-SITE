Themes

TASI 2026 will convene leaders across government, industry, civil society and academia to examine the most urgent questions shaping AI governance and digital trust. The Festival's themes reflect emerging risks, regulatory priorities and opportunities for inclusive innovation in a rapidly evolving technological landscape.

What We'll Examine

Nine interconnected themes spanning policy, technology, and human impact — each designed to move the conversation from dialogue to action.

01

AI Governance and Regulatory Frameworks

Building ethical, accountable, and inclusive AI frameworks rooted in transparency, multi-stakeholder input, and global cooperation — with a lens on India's emerging regulatory landscape.

AI Policy
02

Safety by Design and Platform Accountability

Embedding safety and accountability into technologies from the outset — exploring how platforms can pre-emptively prevent harm rather than reactively moderate it.

Platform Governance
03

Online Gender Based Violence and Digital Safety

Addressing technology-facilitated gender-based violence (TFGBV) — from image-based abuse and stalkerware to digital coercive control — with survivor-centred policy responses.

TFGBV
04

Fraud, Scams and Financial Exploitation

Combating digital fraud through cross-industry intelligence sharing, rapid-response tracing, and consumer-first protections — spotlighting India's unique fraud ecosystem.

Consumer Safety
05

Mental Health and Digital Wellbeing

Recognising emotional labour in moderation teams, addressing algorithmic harm to youth mental health, and designing digital spaces that support rather than undermine wellbeing.

Wellbeing
06

AI for Inclusive Development

Contextualising AI's potential for India's 1.4 billion people — exploring how AI can power equitable access to services while guarding against algorithmic bias and exclusion.

Inclusion
07

Global South Leadership in AI Governance

Positioning India and the Global South not as rule-takers but as rule-makers — building coalitions, exporting models, and ensuring diverse voices shape the global AI governance agenda.

Global Policy
08

Cross Border Digital Harms and Cooperation

Tackling harms that cross borders — from CSAM networks to coordinated disinformation — and building the mutual legal assistance, data-sharing, and diplomatic frameworks needed to respond.

International
09

Transparency, Risk Assessment and Compliance

Moving beyond tick-box compliance toward meaningful transparency — exploring audit regimes, algorithmic risk assessments, and how regulators and platforms can co-develop accountability standards.

Compliance
